Paths to Power

About Paths to Power

Paths to Power is a collaborative project funded by the African American Action Fund’s ‘Places of Black Women Activism’ grant, dedicated to preserving and uplifting spaces of Black resistance, community-building, and joy during the Civil Rights era. The project offers three key ways to engage:

  • For stewards of historic spaces: You can apply for a grant to digitize your archives, preserving materials that document the significance of places tied to Black women and femmes who were activists during the Civil Rights era.
  • For the public and researchers: Explore the digitized archives online, gaining access to a wealth of information about the physical and cultural landscapes that shaped transformative movements.
  • For community members and history enthusiasts: Join one of our walking tours, which brings these stories to life by connecting participants with the spaces and narratives that define this powerful history.
