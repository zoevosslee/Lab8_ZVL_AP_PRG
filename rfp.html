Overview

Paths to Power is a collaborative project mapping and celebrating Brooklyn’s Civil Rights legacy through spaces connected to Black women activists—places where resistance, community-building, and joy flourished. The initiative seeks to preserve these historic sites while enhancing their accessibility through digitized archives, interactive digital platforms, and crowd-sourced narratives, ensuring their stories resonate with today’s audiences.

To support this mission, we invite applications from institutions, community organizations, and property owners who manage or represent places of historical significance to Black women activists across the United States. Selected applicants will receive grant funding to digitize archives, preserve, and interpret these sites, focusing on public engagement and community involvement.

Grant Focus

  • Digitize and Preserve Archives: Enhance digital accessibility by scanning, cataloging, and preserving records, photographs, or artifacts that document the history of Black women activists and their sites.
  • Engage the Community: Involve local residents and youth in preserving these stories through workshops or interactive programming, encouraging community participation in the digitization and storytelling process.
  • Contribute to the Paths to Power Mapping Tool: Prepare your digitized archives for integration into the Paths to Power platform. This includes formatting files appropriately, providing detailed information to accompany each item, and submitting additional details about your site to be featured on the public-facing website.

Note: Grantees will have access to a digital archivist specialist who will provide technical support and guidance on best practices for digitization, data storage, and metadata capturing. This ensures your project adheres to professional archival standards and maximizes the impact of your digital contributions.

Eligibility

Eligible applicants include:

  • Non-profit organizations, community groups, and historical societies
  • Cultural institutions, libraries, and archives
  • Property owners or managers of historically significant sites
  • Schools, universities, and educational institutions connected to relevant history or sites

Application Requirements

Applicants must submit:

  • Site Overview: Describe the historic site, its significance to Black women activists, and its role within your city’s Civil Rights legacy.
  • Project Proposal: Detail how you plan to use grant funding for digitizing archives, preserving, and/or interpreting the site’s history, including any specific programs or activities.
  • Community Engagement Plan: Outline how you will involve the community, particularly local youth, in learning about and preserving the site’s legacy.
  • Digital Integration Plan: Include specifics on how you will digitize and integrate archival records or narratives into Paths to Power’s digital platform.
  • Budget Proposal: Provide a clear breakdown of how grant funds will support your project components.

Evaluation Criteria

Applications will be evaluated on:

  • Historical Significance: The site’s relevance to the legacy of Black women activists and its role in Civil Rights history.
  • Community Engagement: The project’s potential to educate and inspire local communities, particularly youth.
  • Feasibility and Sustainability: The practicality of the project and potential for lasting impact beyond the grant period.
  • Alignment with Paths to Power: The project’s contribution to preserving, amplifying, and connecting the stories of Black women activists with Brooklyn’s present-day communities.
