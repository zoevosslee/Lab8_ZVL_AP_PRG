Activists Who Made Their Marks Here

Image Source: Prints and Photographs Division, Library of Congress

Activists

Shirley Chisholm (1924-2005)

Shirley Chisholm, a pioneering politician, educator, and activist, was the first Black woman elected to the United States Congress and the first Black candidate to seek a major party's nomination for President. Shirley Chisholm graduated from Brooklyn Girls High School in 1942, where she excelled academically. She served as vice president of the Junior Arista Honor Society and graduated with an academic diploma and a medal of excellence in French.

Image Source: Vanity Fair

Activists

Lena Horne (1917-2010)

Lena Horne, an iconic singer, actress, and civil rights activist, was celebrated for her exceptional talent and her dedication to using her influence for social change. Beyond her artistic achievements, Horne was deeply involved in advocating for civil rights, supporting organizations such as the NAACP. Lena attended Brooklyn Girls High School and took dance lessons there, showing early promise as a performer. At the age of 16, she dropped out to work as a chorus girl at the famed Cotton Club, stepping into the entertainment world to support her mother financially.
