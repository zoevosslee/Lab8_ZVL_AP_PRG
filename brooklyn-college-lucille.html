Lucille graduated in Economics from Brooklyn College in 1963, attending night classes while balancing her public service career, which began in 1949 as a clerk in the Department of Social Services. She was deeply involved in Bedford-Stuyvesant through the Central Brooklyn Coordinating Council, working alongside fellow activists like Shirley Chisholm, Elsie Richardson, and Almira Kennedy Coursey.
