Shirley Chisholm, 1924-2005

Shirley Chisholm, a pioneering politician, educator, and activist, was the first Black woman elected to the United States Congress and the first Black candidate to seek a major party's nomination for President.

Shirley Chisholm graduated from Brooklyn Girls High School in 1942, where she excelled academically. She served as vice president of the Junior Arista Honor Society, a rare achievement for Black girls at the time, as they were often excluded from honors programs. Chisholm graduated with an academic diploma and a medal of excellence in French.
