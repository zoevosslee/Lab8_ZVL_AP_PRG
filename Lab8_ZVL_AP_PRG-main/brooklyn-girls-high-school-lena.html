Lena Horne, 1917-2010

Lena Horne, an iconic singer, actress, and civil rights activist, was celebrated for her exceptional talent and her dedication to using her influence for social change. She became one of the first Black performers to achieve international stardom, known for her captivating performances on stage and screen. Beyond her artistic achievements, Horne was deeply involved in advocating for civil rights, supporting organizations such as the NAACP.

Lena attended Brooklyn Girls High School and took dance lessons there, showing early promise as a performer. At the age of 16, she dropped out to work as a chorus girl at the famed Cotton Club, stepping into the entertainment world to support her mother financially.
